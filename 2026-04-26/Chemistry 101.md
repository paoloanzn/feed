### Main Topic: Production Costs

**Main Thesis:** Disruptions to the basic links in how materials are made cause real incomes to shrink and raise the cost of everyday goods.

**Simple Summary:** The article explains how a simple material like sulfur (from oil and gas) is turned into sulfuric acid, which is needed for many things like fertilizers and batteries. The author argues that conflicts or government actions can break these supply chains, which leads to higher costs for everything we buy, from food to utilities.

**The Bottom Line:** > When the basic flow of materials is broken, it directly increases the cost of living for everyone.
