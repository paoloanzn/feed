### Main Topic: Cultural Preservation

**Main Thesis:** Societies must actively protect unique cultural traditions and languages because the variety and contrast between experiences are what give life and meaning to those traditions.

**Simple Summary:** The author argues that modern societies often prioritize individual gain over honoring ancestral traditions, leading to a loss of unique cultural practices. While progress naturally causes cultural differences to fade away, the author suggests that this loss is a tragedy. To prevent this, people must recognize that the differences and contrasts between cultures are valuable, and therefore, they have a responsibility to actively protect unique traditions and languages.

**The Bottom Line:** > Losing unique cultural practices and languages is a real loss, and we should prioritize preserving them because the variety of human experience is what makes life meaningful.
