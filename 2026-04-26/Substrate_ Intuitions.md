### Main Topic: Computation Context

**Main Thesis:** The physical environment or structure a system uses (the substrate) fundamentally changes how computations are performed, even if the math remains the same.

**Simple Summary:** The article introduces the idea of "substrate," which is the physical context—like where data is stored in a computer's memory or how a calculation is physically done—that affects the result. Just as numbers written on separate pieces of paper perform differently based on their location, data stored in different parts of a computer's memory (like fast cache vs. slower memory) causes noticeable differences in speed and performance. This difference matters when systems become very complex.

**The Bottom Line:** > Understanding the substrate is crucial because the physical setup of a system determines not only how fast it runs but also its security, stability, and overall performance at scale.
