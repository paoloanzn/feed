### Main Topic: Social Group Selection

**Main Thesis:** How people connect with each other (their social network) is more important than just the variety of cultures when determining which social norms and values a group adopts.

**Simple Summary:** The article explores how groups of people decide what is considered important or prestigious. The author argues that the way people interact and copy behaviors—their social network structure—is a key factor in this process, not just how many different cultures exist. Specifically, the way people cluster together in their social groups (how tightly connected they are) strongly influences which behaviors are selected by the group.

**The Bottom Line:** > The structure of social connections, rather than just the number of different cultures, is a powerful force that shapes our shared beliefs and social rules. Understanding these connections helps explain why groups adopt certain norms.
