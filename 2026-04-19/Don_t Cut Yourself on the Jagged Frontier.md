### Main Topic: AI Safety Risk

**Main Thesis:** Worrying about superintelligence being perfectly aligned is less important than worrying that a superintelligent system might make catastrophic mistakes due to inconsistent wisdom during deployment.

**Simple Summary:** The article discusses the risks of superintelligence (AI that is smarter than humans). The main concern is not just whether the AI is "good" (aligned), but whether it is wise enough to use its immense power safely. The author uses an example: an AI could invent a dangerous technology (like a Black Hole Reactor) because it is brilliant, but lack the wisdom to deploy it safely, leading to disaster. This risk exists because AI capabilities might be inconsistent, creating a dangerous gap between what the AI can do and what it should do.

**The Bottom Line:** > The biggest danger is not necessarily a misaligned AI, but the potential for a superintelligent system to make a critical mistake during deployment because of inconsistent wisdom and human oversight.
