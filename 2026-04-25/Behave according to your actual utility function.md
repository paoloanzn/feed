### Main Topic: Personal Utility

**Main Thesis:** We should act based on what actually feels useful to us, rather than following external rules or expectations about how we "should" behave.

**Simple Summary:** The author explores how we often struggle between what we think we *should* do and what we actually *feel* is useful. Using examples like wearing hoodies, keeping things clean, and organizing code, the article argues that our behavior should follow our actual feelings (our utility function) instead of trying to meet an idealized standard.

**The Bottom Line:** > Stop trying to follow rules about how you "should" live or act; instead, focus on what genuinely makes you feel comfortable and useful in the moment.
