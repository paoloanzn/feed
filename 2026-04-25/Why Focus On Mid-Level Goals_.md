### Main Topic: Goal structure

**Main Thesis:** Human behavior is organized in a hierarchy, and cultural coordination naturally evolved at the mid-level goals, which creates tension when trying to manage these norms through higher-level thinking.

**Simple Summary:** Our plans are organized in layers, from very small actions to big goals. Small, low-level actions are easy to manage and automate. However, the middle layer of goals is where we consciously coordinate, set social rules, and assign blame. While we learn these social rules unconsciously, we struggle to make them into clear, high-level goals. The article suggests that while large organizations (like corporations) can manage high-level goals effectively, this often conflicts with the social norms we have evolved, and these norms are now threatened by changing environments.

**The Bottom Line:** > To maintain social stability, we need strong collective agreement, but the current environment and cultural changes are causing these necessary social norms to decay.
