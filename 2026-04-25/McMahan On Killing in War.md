### Main Topic: War Morality

**Main Thesis:** The way we currently judge the morality of killing in war is flawed because it incorrectly separates the morality of starting a war from the morality of fighting it.

**Simple Summary:** Philosopher Jeff McMahan argues that we wrongly assume soldiers fighting in an unjust war have no moral right to act. He claims that the rules for fighting in a war should follow the same basic moral standards as everyday life. He suggests that even if a war is unjust, soldiers still have a right to defend themselves, and this idea should prevent wars from happening in the first place.

**The Bottom Line:** > To make wars more just, we must recognize that the right to defend oneself is a fundamental moral principle that applies equally to both the attackers and the defenders.
