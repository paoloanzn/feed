### Main Topic: Political Populism

**Main Thesis:** Donald Trump's populist efforts failed to challenge the ruling class because this type of politics is fundamentally designed to support, rather than dismantle, the existing government structure.

**Simple Summary:** Trump promised to fight the corrupt ruling class, but he did not succeed because the powerful groups that control the government easily countered his agenda. The article argues that the ruling elites—including government agencies and powerful interest groups—have successfully maintained control by ensuring that political conflicts remain a sideshow. This style of populism is not truly anti-system; instead, it is "pro-regime" because it accepts the current government and seeks only to gain more power within it, which ultimately perpetuates the status quo.

**The Bottom Line:** > True opposition to the government requires rejecting the regime's legitimacy and working to dismantle the state, rather than simply complaining about the system.
