### Main Topic: Military Allegiance

**Main Thesis:** The true obligation of military officers is to the Constitution, not to a specific person or administration, and treating the oath as a mere formality is an institutional fraud.

**Simple Summary:** The article uses the story of *Billy Budd* to argue that the loyalty of military officers should be to the law (the Constitution) rather than to the specific leaders in power. It explains that an officer's oath is not a promise to a person, but a public commitment to the Constitution. When leaders treat this oath as optional, it undermines the entire legal structure and allows institutions to act without proper constitutional checks.

**The Bottom Line:** > Officers must understand that their duty is to the Constitution, not just the current government, because this distinction is essential for maintaining the rule of law and preventing institutions from acting outside of established rules.
