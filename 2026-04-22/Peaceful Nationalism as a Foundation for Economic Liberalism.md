### Main Topic: Nationalism and Economics

**Main Thesis:** Liberal nationalism, which is based on peace and individual freedom, is compatible with economic liberalism.

**Simple Summary:** The article explores the tension between free markets (economic liberalism) and national identity (nationalism), particularly in the context of immigration and border control. It uses the ideas of economist Ludwig von Mises to argue that nationalism is not inherently opposed to economic freedom, provided it is understood as a peaceful force that defends individual liberty against tyranny. The core idea is that a nation can be proud and unified while still supporting free trade and the freedom of its people.

**The Bottom Line:** > Nationalism is compatible with economic freedom when it focuses on self-determination and peace, acting as a defense against oppression rather than seeking to create a centralized state.
