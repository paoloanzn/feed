### Main Topic: AI Risk

**Main Thesis:** Using the idea of "marginal risk" is a misleading excuse that allows people to continue building dangerous AI systems by focusing only on small, individual contributions.

**Simple Summary:** The author argues that focusing on "marginal risk" (the small risk caused by one person or company) is a flawed way to discuss AI safety. While it might seem okay because others are also acting recklessly, it ignores the fact that building one more dangerous system still carries significant, real-world risk. Furthermore, when many developers act this way, the total risk multiplies, making the "marginal risk" argument a way to justify incremental steps toward catastrophe.

**The Bottom Line:** > Don't use the idea of marginal risk to excuse building dangerous AI; it is a tactic that allows people to ignore the true, collective danger of the situation.
