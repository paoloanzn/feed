### Main Topic: Legal Method

**Main Thesis:** When legal methods are merged without structure, the rules meant to limit judicial power lose their function and become tools for arbitrary control.

**Simple Summary:** The article argues that in law, disagreeing about outcomes is fine, but abandoning the methods used to reach those outcomes is a major problem. It uses examples of legal concepts like proportionality and reasonableness to show how different legal traditions approach limiting power. When these methods are mixed without their original structure, they stop acting as limits and instead become ways to justify decisions, which risks turning the law into a tool for unchecked power rather than a system of constraint.

**The Bottom Line:** > If legal methods lose their structure, the law stops being a system of limits and becomes a flexible tool for power. This fusion risks creating arbitrary decisions instead of ensuring fairness.
