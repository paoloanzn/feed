### Main Topic: US Military Power

**Main Thesis:** The United States became the world's dominant military power not by accident, but because a historical shift away from anti-militaristic ideals allowed war to become the engine that created and expanded state power.

**Simple Summary:** The author argues that the U.S. transformed into a massive military and imperial power because it abandoned an older tradition that distrusted war. The core idea is that war is not just a policy option; it is the main force that causes governments to grow stronger, more centralized, and more powerful. This process started with early conflicts and was later driven by political ambition and economic interests, leading the U.S. to become an empire.

**The Bottom Line:** > The current military and imperial status of the U.S. is the result of a historical choice, and understanding this link between war and state power is essential for addressing modern global dynamics.
