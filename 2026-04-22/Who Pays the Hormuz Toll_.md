### Main Topic: Oil Toll Economics

**Main Thesis:** A toll on oil passing through the Strait of Hormuz will ultimately shift the financial burden onto the owners of the oil wells in the Gulf states, forcing them to share revenue with Iran.

**Simple Summary:** The article analyzes a hypothetical toll Iran might place on oil passing through the Strait of Hormuz. While the shippers pay the toll immediately, the long-term economic effect (tax incidence) falls on the producers—the governments of the Gulf states (like Saudi Arabia and the UAE). This means the value of the oil wells themselves will decrease, forcing the ruling classes in the Gulf to share their oil income with Iran. The toll is unlikely to cause a global oil price spike because the Gulf oil is cheap enough to absorb the cost without stopping production.

**The Bottom Line:** > The toll is less about disrupting the global oil market and more about forcing the Gulf states to pay for hosting Iran's enemy, which could weaken the influence of the petro-dollar.
