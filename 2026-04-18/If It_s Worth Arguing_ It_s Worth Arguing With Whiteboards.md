### Main Topic: Productive Disagreement

**Main Thesis:** To have a productive disagreement, you must separate what you observe from what you infer, and use a structured method to compare these points.

**Simple Summary:** People often disagree because they make hidden assumptions about what others believe. To fix this, instead of arguing about big claims, list down only the facts you both agree on (observations). Then, list the conclusions you draw from those facts (inferences). By comparing these lists, you can see exactly where the real disagreement is, making it easier to find common ground and make progress.

**The Bottom Line:** > Use a whiteboard to list observations and inferences before arguing; this method helps you find where you actually disagree and leads to better understanding.
