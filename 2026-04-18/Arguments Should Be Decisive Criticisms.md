### Main Topic: Critical Thinking

**Main Thesis:** To think critically, we must focus on arguments that can definitively prove or disprove something, rather than just accepting ideas that are merely compatible.

**Simple Summary:** This article explains a way to improve critical thinking by separating arguments into two types: decisive (clear win/loss) and indecisive (ambiguous). It argues that we should focus on negative arguments (criticisms) because they are easier to find errors in than positive arguments (stating good traits). The author details how to convert vague arguments into clear criticisms, emphasizing that true knowledge comes from finding definite errors rather than just finding things that seem compatible.

**The Bottom Line:** > To make good decisions, focus on finding clear errors and contradictions in your ideas, because accepting something just because it doesn't contradict other ideas is not enough to guarantee correctness.
