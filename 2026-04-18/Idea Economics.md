### Main Topic: Idea credit

**Main Thesis:** People struggle to value ideas because they don't execute them, creating a conflict over who gets credit for valuable work.

**Simple Summary:** The article discusses the difficulty of getting credit for ideas versus the actual work of executing them. While the saying "ideas are cheap" is common, the author argues that execution is what truly creates value. If you can execute an idea yourself, you get more value. To get credit when you can't execute, you need to build a public reputation or generate momentum first. Ultimately, focusing on trust and reputation is often a better way to handle shared benefits than trying to measure every positive outcome.

**The Bottom Line:** > To get credit for an idea, focus on building your public reputation and generating momentum, as execution is what truly matters, and trust often works better than trying to measure every benefit.
