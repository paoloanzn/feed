### Main Topic: Ethics and Evolution

**Main Thesis:** Objective ethics can exist even if behavior is explained by evolution, because ethical actions increase fitness through their helpfulness, not just accidental features.

**Simple Summary:** The article explores whether evolution by natural selection can explain moral behavior. The author looks at the idea that if behavior is adaptive (helps an organism survive and reproduce), it spreads. The argument suggests that even if evolution explains *how* we behave, it doesn't erase the reality of objective moral truths. The author uses the example of math to show that recognizing truths (like math truths or ethical truths) is a capacity that evolves, and this capacity reveals deeper structures.

**The Bottom Line:** > Moral behavior is linked to increasing fitness (helping others), and this connection is what makes ethical actions truly ethical, regardless of the evolutionary process that spread them.
