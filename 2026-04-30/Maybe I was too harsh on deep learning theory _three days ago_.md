### Main Topic: Deep Learning Theory

**Main Thesis:** Different mathematical ways of looking at infinitely wide neural networks reveal that Mean Field Theory provides a better explanation for how networks actually learn features.

**Simple Summary:** The author explores different theoretical limits used to study very large neural networks. One method (Neural Tangent Kernel) treats these networks as simple linear math, which doesn't explain how they learn useful features. A different method (Mean Field Theory) shows that networks can actually learn features by evolving their internal structure during training. The author concludes that Mean Field Theory offers a more accurate way to understand the learning process in deep networks.

**The Bottom Line:** > To truly understand how deep learning works, we need to look beyond simple mathematical limits and focus on theories like Mean Field Theory, which show that networks learn features rather than just performing basic calculations.
