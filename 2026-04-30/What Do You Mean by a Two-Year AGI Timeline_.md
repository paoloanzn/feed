### Main Topic: AGI Timelines

**Main Thesis:** People often use vague terms for AGI timelines, and it is important to define exactly what kind of time measurement they are using.

**Simple Summary:** When people talk about when AGI will arrive, they often use vague terms like "two years," which can be confusing. The article explains that there are different ways to calculate a timeline, such as the average expected time (mean) or the middle point (median). If you consider the possibility that AGI might never happen, the simple average time becomes mathematically undefined.

**The Bottom Line:** > To avoid confusion, always specify whether you are talking about the average time, the most likely time, or a specific percentage, so everyone understands what the timeline actually means.
