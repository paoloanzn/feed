### Main Topic: Free Speech Rights

**Main Thesis:** The author argues that treating free speech as a simple right to non-discrimination weakens the fundamental concept of individual liberty and self-ownership.

**Simple Summary:** This article discusses a Supreme Court case where the court focused on whether laws discriminate based on a person's viewpoint. The author argues that by framing free speech primarily as a non-discrimination rule, the court is moving away from protecting individual freedom. The core point is that while the government shouldn't discriminate, treating all speech equally can hide the fact that some forms of speech are more important than others, which undermines true freedom.

**The Bottom Line:** > Protecting free speech requires focusing on individual liberty rather than just equality, because allowing the government to decide what views are acceptable erodes personal freedom.
