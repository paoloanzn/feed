### Main Topic: Solving Debate

**Main Thesis:** Trying to use formal methods to map arguments is an inefficient and flawed way to handle real-world disagreements.

**Simple Summary:** Many projects attempt to "solve debate" by breaking arguments down into logical steps, but the author argues this approach fails because real arguments are rarely simple or strictly logical. Arguments usually depend on weighing complex costs, benefits, and values, not just finding a single correct premise. Furthermore, people don't seek formal argument structures; they seek validation or emotional reaction, making these mapping tools useless for convincing others.

**The Bottom Line:** > Formal methods for mapping arguments are too complicated and inefficient for real life, and they do not actually help people resolve disagreements.
