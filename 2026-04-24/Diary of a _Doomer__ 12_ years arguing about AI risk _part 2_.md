### Main Topic: AI Safety

**Main Thesis:** Despite early warnings about AI risks, the mainstream AI research community largely dismissed these concerns, creating a rift between safety advocates and the broader field.

**Simple Summary:** Early advocates for AI safety, like Stuart Russell, raised concerns about the potential dangers of superintelligent AI, but many researchers minimized these risks, treating them as too far in the future. This created a conflict between those focused on AI safety and those focused on AI ethics. The author argues that this lack of serious concern is a distraction, and the real change will come when new technologies, like Large Language Models, prove these safety problems are very real.

**The Bottom Line:** > The public and academic community must stop treating AI safety as a distraction and recognize that the potential risks of uncontrolled AI are real and require immediate, serious attention.
