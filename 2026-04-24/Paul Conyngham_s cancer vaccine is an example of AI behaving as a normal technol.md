### Main Topic: AI and Cancer

**Main Thesis:** A real-life story of a personalized mRNA vaccine for a dog shows that AI is a useful tool in developing complex treatments, but the hype about AI curing cancer is exaggerated.

**Simple Summary:** The article discusses how entrepreneur Paul Conyngham developed a custom mRNA vaccine for his dog's cancer using AI and scientific research. mRNA vaccines work by teaching the body's immune system to attack cancer cells. Personalized vaccines are promising because they target unique cancer markers (neoantigens) specific to an individual, offering a better chance for success than general treatments. While AI helped with the complex calculations needed to design this vaccine, the process still required human expertise and lab work. The author is skeptical that AI alone will revolutionize medicine, emphasizing that real progress comes from precise targeting and human effort.

**The Bottom Line:** > AI is a powerful tool that can help speed up medical research, but it cannot replace the need for human expertise or eliminate the complexity of cancer treatment. Be skeptical of hype, and always seek foundational biological knowledge when evaluating medical claims.
