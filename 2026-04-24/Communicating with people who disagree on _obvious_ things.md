### Main Topic: Communication Assumptions

**Main Thesis:** People often use "obvious" statements to exclude others or force agreement, which creates barriers to honest and good-faith conversation.

**Simple Summary:** When people assume things are "obvious," it can make others feel excluded or stupid, especially in specialized discussions. This happens because what is obvious to one person is not obvious to another. To have good conversations, both speakers and listeners should treat each other with kindness, recognize that people come from different backgrounds, and use clear language to avoid unnecessary assumptions.

**The Bottom Line:** > To improve communication, always assume good intent, be aware of different viewpoints, and focus on asking questions rather than assuming what others know.
