### Main Topic: Immunity and Sickness

**Main Thesis:** Small-scale data suggests that immunity does not build significantly based on limited observation, and individual differences in sickness rates are minor.

**Simple Summary:** The author looked at data from two children to see if immunity builds up over time. One child was isolated for a long period, and the other was not. The study suggests that while the environment (like lockdowns) affects how often people get sick, the difference in sickness rates between the two children was not statistically significant. This means that genetics and upbringing have a small effect on how often a person gets sick.

**The Bottom Line:** > Immunity does not build up simply by being exposed to illness, and the differences in sickness rates between people are usually due to individual factors rather than a strong effect of immunity.
