### Main Topic: AI Framing

**Main Thesis:** Treating AI as something to raise rather than defend against opens up new possibilities for collective responsibility, but the author misidentifies who holds the actual responsibility for AI development.

**Simple Summary:** The article critiques how people talk about AI, arguing that focusing on fear limits our understanding of the technology. The author suggests we should view AI as something we are raising, which calls for people to take responsibility for shaping the systems. However, the author also points out that the proposed solutions and the identification of who is responsible (the "parents") are flawed and ineffective.

**The Bottom Line:** > The real responsibility for AI lies with the people who build and deploy the systems, not just the end-users, and relying on public action alone is not enough to fix structural problems.
