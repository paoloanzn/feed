### Main Topic: Defining Freedom

**Main Thesis:** True freedom is not found in political protest or dissent, but in the practical ability of ordinary people to fix and control the complex systems that govern their lives.

**Simple Summary:** The author argues that focusing on political issues like protest and dissent misses the point of freedom. Instead, real freedom is about having the practical ability to fix things—like a car—and understand how complex technology works. The article uses the difficulty of repairing a modern car to show how modern systems (like car software and chips) are controlled by distant, powerful entities, making it nearly impossible for ordinary people to gain control over their own lives.

**The Bottom Line:** > Freedom means having the power to act upon the world, and this power is lost when complex technology is controlled by invisible, distant systems.
