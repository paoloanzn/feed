### Main Topic: Math philosophy

**Main Thesis:** Mathematical objects are defined by their relationship to other structures, not by existing as abstract things.

**Simple Summary:** The author discusses a view called Modal Structuralism, which suggests that mathematical objects (like the number 2) don't exist on their own; they only exist because of their position within a larger system of rules (a mathematical structure). This means we talk about mathematical truth by imagining what would be true if those structures existed. The author also suggests that our ability to do math evolved because our brains developed logic and counting skills, which helped us survive in a consistent physical universe.

**The Bottom Line:** > Mathematics is based on logical structures, and our ability to understand math is a result of our evolution in a consistent physical world.
