### Main Topic: Moral Blind Spots

**Main Thesis:** Most people are most wrong about their moral beliefs because they lack the data and perspective needed to judge which moral systems are truly effective.

**Simple Summary:** The article outlines seven ways people can be "blinded" when forming opinions, such as relying on group pressure or having poor evidence. The author argues that the biggest mistake people make is failing to consider how adaptive their group's morals are. Since morality is a collective choice, the real challenge is figuring out which moral beliefs lead to the most successful societies, rather than just accepting what their group currently believes.

**The Bottom Line:** > To make better choices, you must stop accepting moral beliefs simply because your group holds them, and instead look for evidence about which moral systems actually lead to success.
